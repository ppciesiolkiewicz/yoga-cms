## Common Key Cutting Mistakes to Avoid

Key cutting may seem simple, but there are a few common mistakes that can lead to problems later on.

One of the most frequent issues is using a worn or damaged key as the base for a copy. This can result in a new key that does not work properly. It is always better to copy from a clean, working key where possible.

Another mistake is choosing poor-quality blanks. Low-quality materials can wear down quickly, affecting how the key works in the lock.

Rushing the job can also lead to inaccurate cuts. A properly cut key should match the original closely and operate smoothly. Taking the time to do it right helps avoid issues such as keys getting stuck or failing to turn.

Working with professional locksmiths reduces these risks and gives you a key you can rely on.

#### [Can you cut keys from a photo?](#)

In some cases, a photo can help identify the key type or fob. This allows us to check stock and confirm if we can provide a copy or replacement. For accurate cutting, we usually need the physical key or access to the lock.

#### [Do I need ID to have a key cut?](#)

For standard house keys, an ID is usually not required. For certain restricted or high-security keys, proof may be needed before a copy can be made. If you are unsure, contact us, and we will advise based on your key type.

#### [Can all keys be duplicated?](#)

Most keys can be duplicated, including house keys, office keys and many fobs. Some restricted keys are designed to limit copying and may require authorisation. Our team can check your key and confirm what is possible.

#### [What happens if a key cannot be copied?](#)

If a key cannot be copied, we can consider other options, such as cutting a new key from the lock or providing a replacement lock with new keys. We will explain the best solution based on your situation so you can choose what works for you.
